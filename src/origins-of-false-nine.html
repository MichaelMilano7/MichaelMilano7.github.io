Austria's national team in 1930

Although many soccer fans believes the false nine was invented by Pep Guaridola and played with Messi, in reality, it was created back in the 1930s. This postion was used by the Austrian national team nicknamed “the wunderteam.” Their false nine was Matthias Sindelar and he was known for dropping back from the striker position and confusing the defenders and midfielders. This team went on to win 14 games in a row for Austria. This false nine position was then used more recenlty by the Barcelona manger Johan Cruyff. He ordered his strikers to drop back and receive passes.

The most famous and succesful example of this position does come from Messi and Guardiola. After 2015 Barcelona experienced such success, many clubs started to implement this position into their tactics such as Liverpool, Real Madrid and Tottenham.
