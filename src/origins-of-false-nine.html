The Wunder Team

Although many soccer fans believes the false nine was invented by Pep Guaridola and played with Messi, in reality, it was created back in the 1930s. This postion was used by the Austrian national team nicknamed “the wonderteam.” Their false nine was Matthias Sindelar and he was known for dropping back from the striker position and confusing the defenders and midfielders. In this image, you can see the formation the team used. At the top is Sindelar and arrows demonstrate his movements. With this new tactic, this team went on to win 14 games in a row for Austria.

Johan Cruyff

This false nine position was then used more recenlty in the 1970s by the Barcelona manger Johan Cruyff. Others argue that Johan Cruyff was the first to use this position. They believe that the role of false nine that the Austria team used was not a true false nine because it drops back on defense rather than offense. Johan Cruyff was the first to implement the offensive movements with his strikers. As a manager he ordered his strikers to drop back and receive passes.

Messi and Guardiola

The most famous and succesful example of this position does come from Messi and Guardiola. The 2015 Barcelona squad managed to score 180 goals in one season breaking the record in La Liga. This team won both the Champions League and finished first in La Liga. After 2015 Barcelona experienced such success, many clubs started to implement this position into their tactics such as Liverpool, Real Madrid and Tottenham.
